Research

Hard-core nonlinear dynamics, grounded in computation and experiments.

My research focuses on non-smooth and piecewise-smooth dynamical systems, hybrid dynamics, impact oscillators, discontinuity-induced bifurcations, and experimental methods for validating mathematical models.

Higher-order maps for non-smooth dynamics

Development and use of mappings across discontinuity boundaries for finite-amplitude perturbations, low-velocity impacts and near-grazing behaviour.

Vibro-impact and flow-induced systems

Dynamics of impacting oscillators and systems where fluid forcing, structural motion and contact interactions create rich nonlinear behaviour.

Experimental nonlinear dynamics

Designing experiments and analog circuits to physically realise nonlinear and non-smooth phenomena rather than treating them only as equations.

Computational and data-driven dynamics

Numerical methods, digital twins, ODE prediction, continuation methods and simulation tools that connect data and dynamical structure.

Limit cycles, phase portraits and impact maps.
