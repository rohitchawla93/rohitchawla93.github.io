About

Physicist by training, engineer by practice, experimentalist by instinct.

I work at the intersection of nonlinear dynamics, non-smooth systems, computational modelling and physical experimentation. The common thread is simple: understand how real dynamical systems behave when ideal smooth assumptions fail.

Research identity

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the UCD School of Mechanical and Materials Engineering, University College Dublin. My work combines analytical, numerical and experimental methods for nonlinear and hybrid dynamical systems.

The systems I care about often involve switching, impacts, friction, contact, discontinuities, grazing and bifurcations — the places where dynamics becomes mathematically rich and physically interesting.

Builder identity

I also build things: experimental rigs, electronic circuits, mechanical demonstrators, CAD assemblies, 3D printed joints, robotic mechanisms and teaching demonstrations.

This part of the site will grow into a project gallery showing prototypes, mechanisms, CAD models, videos, lab notes and open-source code.
