Nonlinear dynamics · experiments · machines

I study dynamical systems by modelling, computing and building them.

I am a researcher working across nonlinear dynamics, non-smooth systems, bifurcation theory, experiments, computation, CAD, electronics and mechanical design. My work sits between physics and engineering: theory when it is needed, experiments when they reveal the real system, and prototypes when an idea has to become tangible.

Selected research directions.

These cards can later expand into full project pages with videos, animations, CAD renders and code.

Non-smooth dynamical systems

Filippov and piecewise-smooth systems, discontinuity boundaries, saltation matrices, transverse discontinuity mappings and local stability.

Impact and grazing dynamics

Vibro-impact oscillators, near-grazing behaviour, higher-order maps, experimental validation and physical implementation.

Experiments and analog systems

Op-amp circuits, mechanical demonstrations, sensors and lab-scale systems that make nonlinear phenomena measurable and visible.

Computational mechanics

Custom solvers, beam models, OpenFOAM implementation and numerical tools for structural and dynamical systems.

Robotics and mechanisms

Robot arms, parallel manipulators, CAD, inverse kinematics, control experiments and ball-balancing platforms.

Data-to-dynamics

Learning dynamics from data, digital twins, prediction of ODEs and hybridising data-driven methods with physical insight.
